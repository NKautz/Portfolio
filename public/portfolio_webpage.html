Bio

In 2011, I graduated from the University of Central Florida with a business degree. My field of study was Finance with a Sport Business Management specialization. Right out of school, I was fortunate that the Miami Marlins baseball club was moving into their own stadium in Little Havana and planning to add 2,000+ new hires. I was able to get my foot in the door with the Sales Department. In one year, I generated hundreds of thousands of dollars in revenue selling season tickets to individual buyers, groups of all sizes and corporate entitites. At the start of my second year with the club, I transitioned into the Customer Service Department. My phone etiquette and knowledge of the CRM & ticketing systems is what lead me to the change. Ten months into that position, however, the Finance Department had massive turnover at the end of the season (late September/early October). That being the case, internally, the club was looking for employees with an accounting/finance background to help (the fiscal year ended on October 31st). I jumped at the chance to try something new and was offered a full-time position with the Finance Department in just a month. Three years later, the grind of accounting took it's toll. After some reflection, I decided to leave the club and work with my dad at his small, accouting/money management firm. During that time, I started to dabble in coding. Soon after, I became really intrigued at all of the possibilities of web development. I researched many coding bootcamps/schools in both South Florida and around the country; ultimately, landing on Wyncode.
